Dataset Overview
Our dataset, dating back to 1988, is comprised of four main databases: Cleveland, Hungary, Switzerland, and Long Beach V. These databases collectively contain 76 attributes, each serving a specific purpose in the diagnosis of heart diseases. The most crucial attribute, the "target" field, indicates the presence of heart disease in the patient, denoted as an integer value where 0 signifies no disease and 1 represents the presence of disease.
Key Attributes
To make precise predictions, our system employs a subset of 14 essential attributes from the extensive dataset. These key attributes include:
Age: The age of the patient in years.
Sex: The gender of the patient (1 for male, 0 for female)
Chest Pain Type: A categorical attribute reflecting the type of chest pain experienced by the patient (values 1-4).
Resting Blood Pressure: The patient's resting blood pressure in mm Hg.
Serum Cholestoral: The serum cholesterol level in mg/dl.
Fasting Blood Sugar: Indicates whether fasting blood sugar is greater than 120 mg/dl (1 for true, 0 for false).
Resting Electrocardiographic Results: Results of resting electrocardiogram (values 0, 1, 2).
Maximum Heart Rate Achieved: The highest heart r ate achieved during testing.
Exercise-Induced Angina: Whether the patient experienced angina due to exercise (1 for yes, 0 for no).
Number of Major Vessels: Indicates the number of major vessels colored by flouroscopy (0-3).
Cardiovascular disease (CVD) refers to a class of disorders that affect the heart and blood vessels, leading to various conditions such as heart attacks, strokes, heart failure, and other related complications. It is one of the leading causes of mortality and morbidity worldwide, posing a significant public health challenge. Cardiovascular diseases can develop due to a variety of factors, including high blood pressure, high cholesterol levels, smoking, obesity, and diabetes. Understanding the risk factors, symptoms, and available prevention strategies is crucial in combating this widespread and potentially life-threatening condition.
We introduce our state-of-the-art AI algorithm for Cardiovascular Disease risk detection, powered by the sophisticated XGBoost Classifier. This cutting-edge machine learning model lies at the heart of our system, enabling us to analyze vast and intricate datasets with unparalleled accuracy. XGBoost excels in discerning subtle patterns within the data, allowing us to identify crucial risk factors associated with Cardiovascular Disease.
Our Model demonstrates impressive precision and recall rates in Cardiovascular Disease risk detection. Through rigorous testing and validation, our algorithm has proven its ability to accurately pinpoint potential risks while minimizing false positives and negatives. This remarkable balance between precision and recall underscores the reliability of our system, instilling confidence in both healthcare professionals and individuals seeking accurate cardiovascular health assessments.
